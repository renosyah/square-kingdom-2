# Square Kingdom Campaign Design

## Philosophy

The campaign is inspired by the Crusades but is **not intended to be a historical simulation**.

History serves as the framework for progression while gameplay and player freedom remain the primary focus.

Players are encouraged to create their own stories through custom factions, unit pools, diplomacy, and army compositions.

---

# Campaign Settings

Before starting a campaign, the player may configure:

* Campaign Name
* Campaign Mission
* Difficulty

Difficulty affects:

* Random event frequency
* AI aggression
* AI attitude modifier

---

# Global Recruitment Pool

The Global Recruitment Pool acts as a shared mercenary pool.

Any unit included in this pool may be recruited by **every faction**.

Default campaigns include only a small selection to preserve faction identity.

Players may freely modify this pool before starting a campaign.

Examples:

* Mercenary Spearmen
* Foreign Crossbowmen
* Exotic Cavalry
* Custom Player Units

---

# Faction Recruitment Pool

Each faction also maintains its own exclusive recruitment pool.

Faction-exclusive units preserve faction identity.

Default campaign factions cannot be modified.

Custom factions may define their own unit pools.

Examples:

## Crusader Expeditionary Force

* Crusader Infantry
* Heavy Knights
* Crossbowmen
* Longbowmen
* Warhammer Infantry

## Tribes of Holy Land

* Camel Riders
* Horse Archers
* Spearmen
* Javelin Skirmishers

## City of Holy Land Defender

* Militia
* Guards
* Archers
* Heavy Spearmen

---

# Default Campaign Factions

The default campaign contains four factions.

* Player Faction
* Crusader Expeditionary Force
* City of Holy Land Defender
* Tribes of Holy Land

Additional factions may be created by the player.

Examples:

* Mongols
* British Empire
* Zulu Kingdom
* Any custom kingdom

---

# Diplomacy

Players may configure diplomacy before starting the campaign.

Possible relationships include:

* Allied
* Neutral
* At War

The three default factions maintain their historical hostility.

Custom factions may have freely configurable diplomacy.

---

# Main Objective

The primary objective remains constant throughout the campaign.

> **Control the City of Holy Land**

The narrative differs depending on which side the player supports.

---

# Campaign Timeline

## Opening Phase

The Crusader Expeditionary Force launches an invasion.

The City of Holy Land Defender is intentionally weaker.

The city is expected to fall into Crusader hands.

This provides players time to establish their own kingdom before the main conflict begins.

---

## Crusader Route

1. Crusaders invade.
2. Crusaders capture the City of Holy Land.
3. Player develops their kingdom.
4. Player launches a campaign to seize the city.
5. Crusaders refuse to surrender.
6. A new crusade begins.

---

## Holy Land Route

1. Crusaders invade.
2. Crusaders capture the City of Holy Land.
3. Player retakes the city.
4. Tribes of Holy Land unite with the player.
5. Player faction becomes:

> **Defender of Holy Land**

6. Another crusade arrives.

---

# Recruitment System

A unit becomes recruitable when all required equipment buildings have been constructed.

Recruitment costs:

* Money
* Population

Technology also determines equipment availability, preventing advanced equipment from appearing early in the campaign.

---

# Sandbox Freedom

Players may completely customize:

* Global unit pool
* Custom factions
* Diplomacy
* Campaign difficulty

This allows campaigns ranging from historically inspired Crusades to entirely fictional scenarios.

Examples:

* Crusaders vs Mongols
* British Empire in the Holy Land
* Zulu Kingdom defending the city
* Peasant armies led by Excalibur-wielding heroes

The campaign is designed to encourage replayability and emergent storytelling rather than strict historical accuracy.
